Programme Structure & Exit Options

The BBA programme is designed with flexibility, allowing you to exit at different stages with a credential or continue for an Honours degree.

After 1 Year: UG Certificate in Business Administration

Earn a certificate by completing the first two semesters (a total of 47 credits).

After 2 Years: UG Diploma in Business Administration

Earn a diploma by completing the first four semesters (a total of 93 credits).

After 3 Years: BBA Degree

Complete six semesters to earn a full BBA Degree. Credit requirements: 136 credits for a Single Major or 144 credits for a Double Major.

After 4 Years: BBA (Honours) / (Honours with Research)

Complete all eight semesters to earn a BBA (Honours) (176 credits) or BBA (Honours with Research) (184 credits). A minimum of 75% aggregate marks till the 6th semester is required for the Research track.

Detailed Core Syllabus

BBA 101: Management Process & Organizational Behaviour

Unit I: Introduction to Management: Concept, Functions, Evolution of Management Thought, MBO.

Unit II: Planning & Organizing: Types of Plans, Strategic Planning, Decision-making, Organizing Concepts, Authority, Delegation, Organization Structures.

Unit III: Organizational Behavior: Personality, Learning, Perception, Values, Attitudes.

Unit IV: Motivation & Leadership: Motivation, Leadership, Conflict, Power, Organizational Culture.

BBA 103: Business Mathematics

Unit I: Principle of Counting, Progression, Functions.

Unit II: Matrix Algebra, Determinants, Systems of Linear Equations, Input-Output Analysis.

Unit III: Differential Calculus, Optimization, Maxima & Minima, Lagrangian Multipliers, Mathematics of Finance.

Unit IV: Integral Calculus, Integration Techniques, Consumer/Producer Surplus, Probability.

BBA 105: Financial Accounting and Analysis

Unit I: Meaning and Scope of Accounting, Principles and Standards (GAAP, IFRS, Ind AS).

Unit II: Journalizing Transactions, Ledger Posting, Trial Balance, Company Final Accounts as per Schedule-III.

Unit III: Depreciation, Provisions and Reserves, Contemporary Issues (Human Resource Accounting, Green Accounting).

Unit IV: Shares and Share Capital, Issue of Debentures, Redemption of Debentures.

BBA 107: Business Economics

Unit I: Introduction to Business Economics, Consumer Behavior (Cardinal and Ordinal Utility).

Unit II: Demand Analysis, Elasticity of Demand, Demand Forecasting, Theory of Production, Cost Analysis.

Unit III: Price Output Decisions under Perfect Competition, Monopoly, Monopolistic Competition, Oligopoly.

Unit IV: Concepts of Macro Economics, National Income concepts and measurement.

BBA 109: Entrepreneurial Mindset

Unit I: Introduction to Entrepreneurship, Theories, Characteristics, Myths, Mindset, Creativity, Innovation.

Unit II: Promotion of a Venture, Opportunity Analysis, External Environment Analysis, Business Plan Writing.

Unit III: Entrepreneurship Support, EDPs, Role of Government, Support Institutions (Central, State, etc.).

Unit IV: Practicals including business plan presentation, startup case studies, and policy discussions.

BBA 102: Marketing Management

Unit I: Introduction to Marketing, Marketing Environment, Consumer Decision Making, Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning.

Unit II: Product Decisions, Branding, New Product Development, PLC, Pricing Decisions & Strategies.

Unit III: Distribution Channels, Channel Management, Promotion Mix, IMC.

Unit IV: Emerging Trends: Relationship Marketing, Sustainable Marketing, Green Marketing, Digital Marketing, AI in Marketing.

BBA 104: Decision Techniques for Business

Unit I: Statistics fundamentals, Frequency Distribution, Measures of Central Tendency and Variation, Normal Distribution.

Unit II: Correlation Analysis (Karl Pearson, Spearman's Rank), Regression Analysis (Ordinary Least Square).

Unit III: Linear Programming: Formulation, Graphical and Simplex methods, Duality.

Unit IV: Transportation and Assignment Problems: Initial Solution, Optimality Testing, Hungarian Method, Travelling Salesman Model.

BBA 106: Human Resource Management

Unit I: Introduction to HRM: Nature, Scope, Functions and Importance. Evolution of HRM.

Unit II: Human Resource Planning, Job Analysis, Job Design, Recruitment and Selection Process.

Unit III: Training and Development, Performance Management and Appraisal Systems.

Unit IV: Compensation Management, Employee Relations, Industrial Disputes, Trade Unions.

BBA 108: Financial Management

Unit I: Introduction to Financial Management, Goals, Financial Decisions, Time Value of Money.

Unit II: Capital Budgeting: Techniques (Payback, NPV, IRR), Risk Analysis.

Unit III: Cost of Capital, Capital Structure Theories, Leverage Analysis.

Unit IV: Working Capital Management: Cash, Inventory, and Receivables Management.

BBA 110: Business Communication

Unit I: Introduction to Business Communication: Process, Principles (7Cs), Types, Barriers.

Unit II: Written Communication: Business Letters, Memos, Reports, Proposals.

Unit III: Oral Communication: Presentations, Meetings, Interviews, Group Discussions.

Unit IV: Non-Verbal Communication, Cross-Cultural Communication, Technology in Communication.

BBA 201: Research Methodology

Unit I: Introduction to Business Research, Research Process, Research Design.

Unit II: Sources and Methods of Data Collection, Questionnaire Design, Sampling Techniques.

Unit III: Data Analysis: Hypothesis Testing (t-test, chi-square), ANOVA.

Unit IV: Report Writing and Presentation, Ethics in Business Research.

BBA 203: Operations Management

Unit I: Introduction to Operations Management, Operations Strategy, Process Design.

Unit II: Facility Location and Layout, Capacity Planning.

Unit III: Quality Management: TQM, Six Sigma, Statistical Quality Control.

Unit IV: Supply Chain Management, Inventory Management Models (EOQ).

BBA 205: Corporate Accounting

Unit I: Accounting for Share Capital and Debentures - Advanced Issues.

Unit II: Amalgamation, Absorption, and External Reconstruction of Companies.

Unit III: Internal Reconstruction and Liquidation of Companies.

Unit IV: Accounting for Holding Companies, Preparation of Consolidated Financial Statements.

BBA 207: Legal and Regulatory Framework

Unit I: The Indian Contract Act, 1872: Essential Elements, Performance, Breach.

Unit II: The Sale of Goods Act, 1930: Contract of Sale, Conditions and Warranties.

Unit III: The Companies Act, 2013: Nature of Company, Memorandum, Articles, Directors.

Unit IV: The Consumer Protection Act, 2019; Introduction to Intellectual Property Rights.

BBA 202: International Business

Unit I: Introduction to International Business, Globalization, Modes of Entry.

Unit II: International Trade Theories, Balance of Payments, WTO.

Unit III: Foreign Exchange Market, Exchange Rate Determination.

Unit IV: International Marketing, International HRM, Global Strategy.

BBA 204: Management Information Systems (MIS)

Unit I: Introduction to MIS, Role of IT in Business, System Concepts.

Unit II: Information Systems in Functional Areas (Marketing, Finance, HR, Operations).

Unit III: Enterprise Systems (ERP, SCM, CRM), Decision Support Systems (DSS).

Unit IV: System Development Life Cycle (SDLC), IT Security and Ethical Challenges.

BBA 206: Cost and Management Accounting

Unit I: Introduction to Cost & Management Accounting, Cost Concepts, Cost Sheet.

Unit II: Marginal Costing and CVP Analysis, Budgeting and Budgetary Control.

Unit III: Standard Costing and Variance Analysis.

Unit IV: Decision Making: Make or Buy, Product Mix, Pricing Decisions.

BBA 208: Business Ethics & Corporate Governance

Unit I: Introduction to Business Ethics, Ethical Theories, Ethical Dilemmas.

Unit II: Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR): Concept, Models, and Stakeholder Theory.

Unit III: Corporate Governance: Concepts, Theories, and Models.

Unit IV: Role of Board of Directors, Committees (Audit, Nomination), Whistle-blower Policy.

BBA 301: Strategic Management

Unit I: Introduction to Strategic Management, Vision, Mission, Objectives.

Unit II: External Analysis (PESTEL, Porter's Five Forces), Internal Analysis (VRIO, SWOT).

Unit III: Strategy Formulation: Business-Level and Corporate-Level Strategies.

Unit IV: Strategy Implementation, Evaluation, and Control.

BBA 303: Project Management

Unit I: Introduction to Project Management, Project Life Cycle, Project Selection.

Unit II: Project Planning, Work Breakdown Structure (WBS), Project Scheduling (PERT/CPM).

Unit III: Project Cost Estimation, Resource Allocation, Risk Management.

Unit IV: Project Monitoring and Control, Project Closure, Agile Project Management.

--- Plus Specialization Elective I & II ---

BBA 302: Entrepreneurship Development

Unit I: Entrepreneurial Process, Business Idea Generation and Feasibility Analysis.

Unit II: Preparing a Business Plan, Legal Forms of Business.

Unit III: Sources of Finance: Venture Capital, Angel Investors, Crowdfunding.

Unit IV: Managing Growth, Scaling Up, and Exit Strategies for Entrepreneurs.

BBA 304: Final Project / Internship Report

Objective: To provide students with hands-on experience in a business environment or to conduct an in-depth research project on a specific business problem.

Components: Company/Industry Analysis, Problem Identification, Data Collection, Analysis, and Recommendations.

Evaluation: Based on a written report and a viva-voce examination.

Finance Electives

Areas of Specialization (Major/Minor)

Students can choose electives to specialize in up to two areas. A Major requires six papers from one area (24 credits), while a Minor requires four papers (16 credits). A Double Major is achieved by completing at least six electives from a second specialization.

Training & Internships

BBA 116: Industrial Skill-Based Training (Semester II)

A mandatory 4-credit course involving a minimum of 60 hours of training. This can be done in segments or as a continuous session, online or in-house, focusing on practical industry skills.

BBA 327: Summer Training / Apprenticeship (After Semester IV)

A mandatory eight-week summer training in an industry, research, or academic institute. A report and viva-voce will be evaluated in the fifth semester.

BBA 412: Research Project/Dissertation (Semester VIII)

For students pursuing BBA (Honours with Research). This 12-credit project requires independent research, a detailed report, and a viva-voce. A publication in a reputed journal/conference is encouraged.
